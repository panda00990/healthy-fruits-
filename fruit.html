BETTER HEALTH

facts
  • Fruits are not only delicious but also fascinating. Did you know that bananas are berries, while strawberries are not? Apples float in water because they're less dense than water. Pineapples contain bromelain, an enzyme that tenderizes meat. Avocados are berries, but not sweet. Watermelons are 92% water. The world's heaviest fruit, the jackfruit, can weigh up to 100 pounds! Fruits truly showcase nature's diversity and wonder.


  • Fruits are vital parts of a balanced diet since they provide so many health advantages. They are high in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals that promote general health. Frequent fruit consumption can lower the chance of developing chronic illnesses like heart disease, stroke, and several types of cancer. Fruits also include a lot of fiber, which helps regulate blood sugar levels, encourages bowel regularity, and facilitates digestion. In addition, fruits' natural sugars offer a rapid energy boost, which makes them perfect for pre- or post-workout snacking. Furthermore, a lot of fruits include hydrating qualities that raise the body's overall hydration levels.




bananas

  • Excellent source of potassium, which helps regulate blood pressure and maintain heart health.
  • Contains vitamin B6, aiding in brain health and neurotransmitter synthesis.
  • Provides quick energy due to its natural sugars.
Oranges

  • High in vitamin C, boosting immunity and promoting collagen production for healthy skin.
  • Contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties and may reduce inflammation.
  • Good source of fiber, aiding in digestion and promoting satiety.
apples

  • Rich in antioxidants like quercetin, which may help reduce the risk of heart disease.
  • High in fiber, promoting digestive health and aiding in weight management.
  • Contains vitamin C, supporting the immune system and skin health.

Berries (Strawberries, Blueberries, Raspberries):

  • Packed with antioxidants, such as anthocyanins, which may help protect against chronic diseases like cancer and heart disease.
  • Rich in vitamins C and K, supporting immune function and bone health.
  • High fiber content promotes digestive health and may help control blood sugar levels.
grapes

  • Contains resveratrol, an antioxidant that may have heart-protective effects and contribute to longevity.
  • Provides hydration due to high water content.
  • Contains vitamin K, which is essential for bone health and blood clotting.
Pinapple

  • Contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that may aid in digestion and reduce inflammation.
  • High in vitamin C, promoting immune function and collagen synthesis.
  • Rich in manganese, supporting bone health and metabolism.
watermelon

  • High water content helps keep you hydrated and supports overall hydration levels.
  • Contains lycopene, a powerful antioxidant that may help reduce the risk of certain cancers and protect against heart disease.
  • Good source of vitamins A and C, which are essential for skin health and immune function.
